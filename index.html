The Philippine Light Rail Line 1 was first introduced in 1984 and traveled a North-South route. Line 2, blue line (formerly purple line), was completed in 2004 and took place in East-West. The original line 1 was built as a public transport without cumbersome and there were no certain features and amenities, but the new line 2 was built with additional standards and criteria in mind such as access without barriers. In 2022, the system served on average 305,264 passengers. [1] The security agents of each station perform inspection and provide support. A reusable magnetic ticket system has replaced the previous notification code in 2001 and Flash Pass was introduced as a step for a more integrated transportation system. In 2015, plastic tickets were replaced by BEEP, an unexplained smart card, introduced to provide a popular ticket office on 3 railways and some bus routes.
